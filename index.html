Project Overview

Bridging Frontier Research & Higher Education

This project unites institutions in the UK and Vietnam to embed frontier research in Digital Twins, Next-Generation Wireless Networks, and Quantum Technologies into higher education curricula.

By developing early-career lecturers' capacity and enhancing students' access to research-informed learning, we aim to strengthen institutional capability to deliver inclusive, internationally competitive ICT programmes aligned with Vietnam's strategic technological priorities.

May 22, 2026

Quantum Cloud Computing and Software Systems: Fundamentals, Practice and Experience.

Abstract

Quantum computing is moving toward practical use, yet heterogeneous, noisy intermediate-scale quantum (NISQ) hardware and fragmented software stacks still make application deployment and resource management challenging. This webinar has two goals: first, to provide a brief overview of quantum computing fundamentals, including qubits, gates, circuits, quantum cloud computing, and current constraints; second, to share practical experience with software systems and frameworks for modelling, simulation, and deploying applications in quantum computing environments, with a focus on quantum software workflows and optimising quantum resource management. We will conclude by outlining open problems and future directions for scalable quantum cloud and software systems, resource orchestration, and distributed execution, and by sharing insights from quantum initiatives in Australia.

Speaker Profile

Dr. Hoa Nguyen

CSIRO, Australia

Dr Hoa Nguyen is a Research Scientist in the Quantum Systems Team at CSIRO, Australia’s national science agency. His expertise lies at the intersection of quantum computing, artificial intelligence, and cloud computing, with research interests in quantum software engineering, quantum machine learning applications, and AI-driven optimisation for resource orchestration in distributed quantum computing systems. Hoa is developing quantum software systems for the National Quantum Computing Testbed (NQCT) in collaboration with the University of Queensland and quantum industry partners. He completed his PhD at the University of Melbourne, focusing on serverless and reinforcement learning-based resource management for quantum cloud computing, and holds bachelor's and master's degrees from the University of Information Technology, Vietnam National University (UIT-HCM). He has over eight years of teaching experience in Vietnam and Australia, and he works with national and international partners to advance scalable quantum computing architectures and software systems that support Australia’s growing quantum ecosystem.

June 16, 2026

Integrated and Intelligent Connectivity for 6G and Beyond: Role of ISAC and Intelligent Metasurfaces

Abstract

Following the IMT-2030 framework by ITU-R, two major technologies of integrated sensing and communications (ISAC) and intelligent metasurfaces (IM), i.e., RIS, holographic MIMO (H-MIMO), herald a new era of 6G connectivity by transcending conventional communication networks to highly energy efficient and ultra-high data rates for smart radio environment, with exploration of frequency bands such as cmWave and sub-terahertz, and multiple use cases for 6G such as simultaneous imaging, mapping and localization, smart healthcare, etc.

Furthermore, considering recent 3GPP Rels. 19 and 20, ISAC will play a vital role in 6G wireless standards, while RIS, H-MIMO, electromagnetic signal and information theory (ESIT) are some of the technologies expected in future 3GPP Rel. 20+. This talk will present a comprehensive overview of emerging ISAC and IM (particularly RIS, H-MIMO, refractive surfaces) based 6G and beyond wireless communications including vision, standardization, fundamentals, challenges, use cases, and synergies between ISAC and IM technologies, and new opportunities.

In terms of emerging designs, this talk will focus on case studies such as RF chain and quantization optimization, ESIT capabilities, index modulation, and IM-aided ISAC for non-terrestrial networks (NTN), and related applications.
